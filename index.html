what is a nucleus?

A nucleus acts as the control center of the cell. It regulates the cell growth and reproduction. The nucleus contains structerescalled chromosomes. Chromosomes contain the cell genes, which determine the characteristic of the cell.

How does a nucleus work

By housing the cell's genome,the nucleus serves both as the repository of genetic information and as the cell's control center. DNA replication,transcription, and RNA processing all take place within the nucleus, with only the final stage of gene expression localized to the cytoplasm.
